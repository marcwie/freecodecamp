Alan Turing

The creator of modern computing

Here's a time line of Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London
  • 1918 - Joins St Michael's day school, Hastings
  • 1926 - Joins Sherborne School, Dorset and starts to develop an interest in Science and Mathematics
  • 1931 - Becomes an undergraduate at King's College, Cambridge to study Mathematics. Graduates with distinction.
  • 1935 - Elected Fellow of King's College
  • 1938 - Joins Princeton University and is awarded a PhD in mathematics
  • 1939 - Joins the Government Codes and Ciphers School
  • 1940 - Develops 'Bombe' for decrypting the messages sent by the Germans using their Enigma machine
  • 1942 - Breaks the German Naval Enigma system
  • 1945 - Awarded the Order of the British Empire for his wartime services
  • 1946 - Joins the National Physical Laboratory and publishes the first design of a stored-program computer
  • 1948 - Reader in the Mathematics Department of Manchester University
  • 1949 - Deputy director of the Computing Laboratory at Manchester University
  • 1950 - Dvelops the Turing Test to define a standard for a machine to be called intelligent
  • 1951 - Fellow of the Royal Society
  • 1952 - Arrested for gross indecency and loss of security clearance. Receives chemical treatment as alternative to imprisonment, which has a detrimental effect on him
  • 1954 - Found poisoned by cyanide in his home in Wilmslow, Cheshire
